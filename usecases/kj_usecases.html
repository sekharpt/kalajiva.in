SICCI Use Cases Aligned with Kalajiva’s Model

1. Flood Protection for Sugarcane and Paddy Farmers (FPOs/SHGs/Bankers)

Scenario:
Late-monsoon floods in Tamil Nadu and Kerala can wipe out standing crops, disrupting not only farmers’ income but also the supply chain for food processors and mills.

Who Pays: The premium is paid by the farmer (potentially subsidized partially by FPO/SHG or bundled with input loans via partner banks).

FPOs/SHGs add real value for their members and can negotiate better bulk rates or receive a referral fee.

Bankers reduce agri-loan defaults (as insured farmers are more likely to repay after a climate shock).

“Imagine if, after the next flood, your farmer members or loan customers could recover in days—not months—without chasing claims.”

2. Post-monsoon Dry Spell Protection for Rabi Sowing

Scenario:
Post-monsoon dry spells in November affect Rabi sowing, leading to lower yields and cash flow issues for both farmers and FPOs.

3. Supply Chain Stability for Retailers via Supplier Insurance (Retail Companies/Bankers/Tech Providers)

Who Pays: Suppliers (farmers/FPOs), perhaps with partial premium support from the retailer as a loyalty incentive.

Scenario:
Heatwaves during October–December can reduce milk yields, causing income loss for dairy farmers and supply shortages for processors.

Who Pays: The dairy farmer (premium often paid at milk collection or via FPO facilitation).

“Dairy supply chains are vulnerable to rising heat—why not protect your farmers and your inventory?”
